Momase Region

Criminal Records Administration for Madang, Morobe, East Sepik, and West Sepik Provinces

Regional Overview

The Momase Region comprises four provinces along Papua New Guinea's northern coastline. This region drives a significant portion of the nation's international trade, with Lae serving as the country's primary industrial port. Law enforcement operations span maritime border management, industrial security, and cross-border surveillance along the Indonesian frontier.

Provinces in the Momase Region

Madang Province

Capital: Madang

A coastal province with a vibrant fishing industry and growing tourism sector. Major international port operations require dedicated maritime law enforcement units.

  • Population: ~440,000
  • Active Cases: 680
  • Police Stations: 26

Morobe Province

Capital: Lae

PNG's most industrialised province and home to the nation's largest port. Lae's manufacturing and logistics activity creates substantial law enforcement requirements around cargo security and industrial crime.

  • Population: ~680,000
  • Active Cases: 1,250
  • Police Stations: 42

East Sepik Province

Capital: Wewak

A northern province with active international trade routes and maritime boundary operations that require ongoing cross-agency coordination.

  • Population: ~450,000
  • Active Cases: 520
  • Police Stations: 21

West Sepik Province

Capital: Vanimo

Bordering Indonesia in the north-west, this province is critical to national border security with dedicated units focused on smuggling prevention and unauthorised crossings.

  • Population: ~280,000
  • Active Cases: 490
  • Police Stations: 18
