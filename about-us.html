About the RPNGC Criminal Record Management Portal

Dedicated professionals building a secure, trusted national criminal records infrastructure

Our Mission

To deliver a secure, dependable, and efficient digital platform for managing criminal records across every region of Papua New Guinea — enabling law enforcement personnel to safeguard communities and uphold national security.

Our Vision

A fully unified national criminal records infrastructure that underpins PNG's law enforcement operations, accessible to authorised personnel in all regions with real-time data and the highest standards of digital security.

About the Royal Papua New Guinea Constabulary

The Royal Papua New Guinea Constabulary (RPNGC) is the national police service of Papua New Guinea, charged with maintaining law and order across every province and region. With more than 8,000 personnel stationed at 156 police stations nationwide, the RPNGC serves a population exceeding nine million citizens.

The Criminal Record Management Portal represents a landmark modernisation effort — transitioning from paper-based legacy systems to a robust digital infrastructure that supports seamless coordination between regional commands and individual police stations throughout the country.

Key Milestones

2023

Project Initiation

The Criminal Record Management Portal initiative was approved and secured funding from the PNG government.

2024

System Development

Full platform development completed, incorporating 256-bit AES encryption and redundant cloud backup systems.

Q1 2025

Pilot Rollout

A successful pilot deployment was completed across National Capital District and Central Province.

Q2 2025

National Deployment

The system went live at all 156 police stations across PNG's 22 provinces.

Q3 2025

Data Migration

Over 50,000 criminal records were successfully transferred from legacy paper and digital archives.

Q4 2025

Analytics Launch

Advanced crime analytics and predictive policing tools were released to all regional commands.

2026

Ongoing Operations

The system maintains 99.8% uptime with real-time criminal record access available across all four regions.
