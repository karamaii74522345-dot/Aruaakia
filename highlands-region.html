Highlands Region

Criminal Records Administration for Eastern Highlands, Simbu, Southern Highlands, Hela, Enga, Western Highlands, and Jiwaka Provinces

Regional Overview

The Highlands Region spans seven provinces across Papua New Guinea's rugged interior. Unique law enforcement challenges stem from geographic remoteness, varied terrain, and intricate tribal governance systems. The region is a major contributor to national GDP through agriculture and extractive industries, creating specific crime pressures around resource management and industrial operations.

Provinces in the Highlands Region

Eastern Highlands Province

Capital: Goroka

An elevated province celebrated for coffee production and cultural diversity, with law enforcement priorities centred on organised crime networks and inter-tribal dispute resolution.

  • Population: ~620,000
  • Active Cases: 580
  • Police Stations: 22

Simbu Province

Capital: Kundiawa

A central mountain province where police focus on mediating long-standing tribal disputes and managing resource allocation conflicts.

  • Population: ~330,000
  • Active Cases: 245
  • Police Stations: 12

Southern Highlands Province

Capital: Mendi

A key agricultural and mining hub where law enforcement maintains a strong presence to counter economic crime and protect industrial operations.

  • Population: ~710,000
  • Active Cases: 892
  • Police Stations: 28

Hela Province

Capital: Tari

One of PNG's newest provinces and home to major oil and gas operations, with enforcement focused on industrial crime and environmental compliance.

  • Population: ~180,000
  • Active Cases: 156
  • Police Stations: 8

Enga Province

Capital: Wabag

A densely populated agricultural province where frequent clan-related violence remains the primary policing challenge for local stations.

  • Population: ~450,000
  • Active Cases: 234
  • Police Stations: 11

Western Highlands Province

Capital: Mount Hagen

Home to PNG's second-largest city, this commercially active province contends with organised crime linked to its significant trading and transport networks.

  • Population: ~520,000
  • Active Cases: 456
  • Police Stations: 19

Jiwaka Province

Capital: Minj

A newly established province in the western highlands region, where police are actively mapping and responding to emerging crime patterns.

  • Population: ~310,000
  • Active Cases: 282
  • Police Stations: 9
