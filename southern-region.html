Southern Region

Criminal Records Administration for National Capital District, Central, Gulf, Milne Bay, Oro, and Western Provinces

Regional Overview

The Southern Region covers six provinces that form the political and strategic core of Papua New Guinea. The National Capital District houses the nation's government and serves as the central coordination hub for law enforcement operations, while the surrounding provinces manage critical maritime borders and cross-country trade corridors.

Provinces in the Southern Region

National Capital District

Capital: Port Moresby

The administrative and political centre of Papua New Guinea. Home to the highest concentration of urban crime cases and the primary national coordination hub for law enforcement.

  • Population: ~380,000
  • Active Cases: 3,200
  • Police Stations: 42

Central Province

Capital: Konedobu

Situated in the south-east, this province is focused on maritime security and enforcement of fishing regulations along its extensive coastline.

  • Population: ~260,000
  • Active Cases: 1,450
  • Police Stations: 28

Gulf Province

Capital: Kerema

A strategically important coastal province where policing efforts concentrate on cross-border trafficking prevention and illegal maritime activity.

  • Population: ~180,000
  • Active Cases: 890
  • Police Stations: 18

Milne Bay Province

Capital: Alotau

An eastern maritime region with dedicated coastal security units and oversight of international border crossings with the Solomon Islands.

  • Population: ~270,000
  • Active Cases: 1,320
  • Police Stations: 32

Oro Province

Capital: Popondetta

Borders the Solomon Islands, with extensive maritime law enforcement duties covering both coastal and inland patrol operations.

  • Population: ~190,000
  • Active Cases: 980
  • Police Stations: 21

Western Province

Capital: Daru

Shares a long international border with Indonesia. Law enforcement priorities centre on cross-border trafficking, smuggling, and illegal entry operations.

  • Population: ~210,000
  • Active Cases: 1,102
  • Police Stations: 15
