New Guinea Islands Region

Criminal Records Administration for East New Britain, West New Britain, New Ireland, Bougainville, and Manus Provinces

Regional Overview

The New Guinea Islands Region encompasses five provinces spread across the eastern and northern archipelagos of Papua New Guinea. The dispersed geography presents distinct maritime law enforcement challenges, including the protection of fisheries, monitoring of international maritime boundaries, and coordination of responses to cross-island criminal networks.

Provinces in the Islands Region

East New Britain Province

Capital: Rabaul

An island province with significant commercial activity in cocoa and copra. The port at Rabaul holds major regional tourism importance, requiring maritime and commercial crime enforcement.

  • Population: ~380,000
  • Active Cases: 510
  • Police Stations: 18

West New Britain Province

Capital: Kimbe

A major palm oil producer where law enforcement addresses environmental compliance issues and industrial crime linked to plantation operations.

  • Population: ~280,000
  • Active Cases: 340
  • Police Stations: 13

New Ireland Province

Capital: Kavieng

Strategically positioned with a critical fishing industry. Maritime security operations are the primary focus, with cooperation alongside Pacific regional partners.

  • Population: ~195,000
  • Active Cases: 280
  • Police Stations: 11

Autonomous Region of Bougainville

Capital: Buka Town

An autonomous post-conflict region undergoing active recovery. Law enforcement efforts prioritise community policing, rehabilitation, and managing mineral resource disputes.

  • Population: ~290,000
  • Active Cases: 490
  • Police Stations: 16

Manus Province

Capital: Lorengau

A remote island province with strategic importance along Pacific shipping lanes. Policing focuses on fisheries protection and monitoring for illegal maritime activity.

  • Population: ~70,000
  • Active Cases: 72
  • Police Stations: 9
